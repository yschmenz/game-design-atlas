## Principle

Removing fail states changes what an open world *is*: from a threat landscape you must
keep up with, into a place that is simply there — explored at your own pace.

## Example

Sable, the whole game. No enemies, no survival pressure, no game-overs. Quests cannot
be failed or permanently lost — you can drop one, lose track of it, and pick it up
again whenever; the task waits for you.

## Why it works

Most open worlds create urgency through threat (combat, survival timers, fail states).
Sable removes the entire category, so the only remaining motivation is curiosity —
pace becomes fully player-owned instead of dictated by the game's pressures.

## How can I reuse it?

Ask of any system: what does this fail state actually buy? If pressure isn't the
point, letting tasks wait instead of die may serve exploration better.
