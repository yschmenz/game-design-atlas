## From the narrated recording (structured transcript)

Jachym's commentary from the session recording, cleaned & structured — detail layer for the observations above.

**Flashback (opening).** The game completely changes visual style *and genre* for flashbacks — instantly distinctive from the present-day sections. Style shift as a narrative signal.

**Level 1 — the road (obs. 1).** Corridor-style driving level; the level design deliberately steps back so dialogue and characters carry the sequence (mechanics were already taught earlier — nothing new competes for attention). At the fork she takes the far right path — **both paths merge again; the choice makes no difference** (a fake choice that preserves the corridor). Passing the bridge changes the environment and with it the pacing. The next destination is shown on the horizon before you reach it — the goal is *seen*, then travelled to.

**Level 2 — blocked path & window (obs. 2).** The cutscene camera is placed *behind a window* — that window is a planted hint: it later becomes the way in. Structure repeats the game's pattern: corridor → enclosed area. The brake is rusted shut → find a tool → the level pulls you toward the left building while the right one holds the window entrance. A small UI dot marks interactive elements throughout the whole game — subtle, and doubles as interior guidance.

**Level 2b — inner yard (obs. 3).** Three buildings, all blocked; verticality is the challenge and the **ladder is the only interactable** in the area. Even if you miss the intended route, walking the yard reveals the hole in the roof — the space itself teaches the solution. The ladder is then needed again for the way back: same space, second use.

**Level 4 — the dog (obs. 4).** Indika **cannot sprint** — frustrating by design; the chase stays close and the tension high. Light marks the exit unambiguously.

**Level 5 — the mill tower (obs. 5).** The prayer mechanic: in distress the space distorts around her; praying pulls it back together. Platforms are split between the two reality-states — a personal platform puzzle, not complicated, satisfying to traverse. Light plays a role here too, and the sequence leans heavily on audio.

**Level 6 — Deníkovka village (obs. 6).** Very detailed environment, deliberately slow pacing — time to explore. The goal (the giant, exaggerated mill) is visible across impassable water; the last building before the dam has an **exaggerated ramp — plausible for the inhabitants, but really there to lead the player onto the water crossing**. A small shed midway confirms you're on the right path; landmarks + repeated dialogue lines keep pointing the direction.

**Level 7 — ice floes (obs. 7).** Physics puzzle: stand on one end of a floe to raise the other into a ramp.
